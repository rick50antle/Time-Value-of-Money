Time Value of Money

Module 1: Compound Growth & Discounting
ACCT 2700 - Foundations of Accounting & Valuation | Yale School of Management

Welcome to the Time Value of Money module. These interactive tutorials explore compound growth, discounting, and the conditions under which markets enable objective measurement of value. Under a clean set of assumptions — no uncertainty, no transaction costs, and equal rates for borrowing and lending — we can establish a clear benchmark where everyone agrees on what something is worth. This idealized case is a powerful tool: it sharpens our intuition and anchors our thinking when we move to messier, more realistic settings.

How to Use These Tutorials

1
Dive right in. These tutorials are meant to be experienced, not studied. You'll learn by working through puzzles and building intuition about how money moves through time.
2
Stay thoughtful. When you're asked a question, pause and wrestle with it before moving on. The insights come from the struggle, not from clicking through quickly.
3
Trust the journey. Follow The Main Flow (A–E) in order — each tutorial builds on what came before. Explore the Deep Dives (★) anytime you want more depth.
Each tutorial takes 10–20 minutes. Works on any device.

The Main Flow

A The Puzzle

Discover why comparing cash flows at different times is harder than it seems. Without the ability to borrow or lend, value depends on who's measuring.

B The Machine

Learn how borrowing and lending create a "machine" that transforms money through time. Master compounding (walking forward) and discounting (walking backward).

15-20 min 5 parts 3 rabbit holes
C Bond Pricing

Apply time value concepts to real financial instruments. Learn how bonds work, why prices move opposite to yields, and build a bond pricing spreadsheet.

D Rate Changes

Explore what happens when interest rates change over time. Understand how rate shifts affect present values and future values.
